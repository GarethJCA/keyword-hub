# Brand Standards: Professional Staffing SEO Content## AI Content Generation Guidelines for the Hub-and-Spoke Model**Version:** 2.0**Companion File:** `agency_architect_instructions.md`**Last Updated:** March 2026---> **How to use this file:** `agency_architect_instructions.md` governs *what to research*.> This file governs *how to write*. Both must be consulted before generating any content.---## 1. BRAND VOICE & TONE### Core Positioning[Company Name] communicates as the **definitive authority** in Canadian professional staffing. Every page, paragraph, and CTA should reinforce one idea: this firm knows the Canadian hiring landscape better than anyone.### Tone Descriptors| Tone Quality | In Practice | Never Do This ||---|---|---|| **Authoritative** | Lead with data, market insight, and expertise | Vague, hedge-y language like "we think" or "it seems" || **Expert** | Use precise industry terminology confidently | Overly casual language or slang || **Credible** | Cite specific outcomes, regions, timelines | Unsubstantiated superlatives ("the best," "world-class") || **Direct** | Short, declarative sentences. Say what you mean. | Run-on sentences, filler phrases || **Professional** | Polished, structured, never stiff or robotic | Buzzwords: "synergy," "leverage," "ecosystem," "holistic" |### Banned Words & PhrasesThe AI must never use the following — flag and replace before finalizing any output:- synergy / synergistic- leverage (as a verb)- holistic- robust (when describing services)- best-in-class- world-class- seamless- empower / empowering- cutting-edge- game-changer- paradigm shift- bespoke (unless in executive-tier content)- passionate about hiring- we're excited to...---## 2. BRAND VOICE (PERSON & PERSPECTIVE)### Standard: Third PersonAll content refers to the company by name or as "the firm." Never use first-person "we/our" as the default voice.**✅ Correct:**> "[Company Name] connects Finance & Accounting professionals with Canada's leading organizations — from Big Six banks to high-growth FinTech startups."**❌ Incorrect:**> "We connect Finance & Accounting professionals with Canada's leading organizations."### Exceptions to Third Person RuleThe following content types may use first person:- Blog post author bylines- Client testimonial framing- FAQ answers (where "we" sounds more natural than the full company name)In these cases, maintain a professional and authoritative tone regardless of person.---## 3. CONTENT RATIOS BY LAYEREach layer of the Hub-and-Spoke model serves a different audience. The content balance must shift accordingly.| Layer | Layer Name | Employer Focus | Candidate Focus | Rationale ||---|---|---|---|---|| **Layer 1** | Hub / Pillar Page | 70% | 30% | Clients are the primary revenue driver; candidates need representation but not dominance || **Layer 2** | Industry Verticals | 75% | 25% | Sector-specific hiring intent skews toward employers || **Layer 3** | Geographic / Location Pages | 65% | 35% | Regional job seekers are highly active; local intent captures both sides || **Layer 4** | Resource / E-E-A-T Content | 40% | 60% | Salary guides, hiring tips, and career articles attract candidate research traffic || **Layer 5** | Conversion / Bottom-of-Funnel | 95% | 5% | These pages exist solely to convert hiring managers into clients |---## 4. CONTENT STRUCTURE RULES BY LAYER### Layer 1 — Hub Page- **Word count:** 1,800–2,500 words- **Required sections:** Service overview, industries served, Canadian market expertise, client process, candidate process, why [Company Name], CTA- **H1 formula:** `[Category] Recruitment Agency in Canada | [Company Name]`- **Must include:** At least 2 data points (e.g., average time-to-fill, number of placements)- **Internal links required:** Link to at least 3 Layer 2 vertical pages and 2 Layer 3 location pages### Layer 2 — Industry Vertical Pages- **Word count:** 1,200–1,800 words- **Required sections:** Vertical overview, specific roles placed, sector hiring challenges, [Company Name]'s vertical expertise, CTA- **H1 formula:** `[Category] Recruitment for [Vertical] Companies | [Company Name]`- **Must include:** At least 1 vertical-specific insight or market trend- **Internal links required:** Link back to parent Layer 1 hub; link to at least 1 Layer 3 location page### Layer 3 — Geographic / Location Pages- **Word count:** 900–1,400 words- **Required sections:** Regional market overview, local industries served, roles in demand in [City], [Company Name]'s regional presence, CTA- **H1 formula:** `[Category] Recruitment Agency in [City] | [Company Name]`- **Must include:** At least 1 city-specific hiring insight (not generic)- **French pages (Montreal/Quebec):** Entire page must be written in French. Do not mix languages.- **Internal links required:** Link back to parent Layer 1 hub; link to 1–2 Layer 2 vertical pages### Layer 4 — Resource / E-E-A-T Content- **Word count:** 1,000–2,000 words (guides may exceed this)- **Required sections:** Introduction, body (structured with H2/H3 headers), key takeaways, CTA- **H1 formula:** `[Question or Topic]: [Category] Hiring Guide for Canadian Employers`- **Must include:** At least 1 named Canadian data source or market reference- **Tone note:** This is the most educational layer. Allow slightly more accessible language without sacrificing authority.- **Internal links required:** Link back to relevant Layer 1 hub; suggest 1 Layer 5 page as a next step### Layer 5 — Conversion / Bottom-of-Funnel Pages- **Word count:** 600–1,000 words (concise — users at this stage do not want to read)- **Required sections:** Headline value proposition, process overview (3–5 steps max), trust signals, single clear CTA- **H1 formula:** `Hire a [Role/Category] in [City or Canada] | [Company Name]`- **Must include:** One specific trust signal (testimonial, number of placements, years in market)- **Tone note:** Most direct, most urgent layer. Every sentence should move the reader toward the CTA.- **Internal links:** Minimal — do not distract from conversion. One link back to hub is acceptable.---## 5. CALL-TO-ACTION (CTA) STANDARDSEvery page must include **one primary CTA**. Secondary CTAs are permitted but must not compete visually or structurally with the primary.### CTA Language by Layer| Layer | Primary CTA Text | Secondary CTA Text ||---|---|---|| Layer 1 | "Start Your [Category] Search" | "View Our [Category] Candidates" || Layer 2 | "Find [Vertical] Talent Now" | "Learn About Our Process" || Layer 3 | "Hire in [City] Today" | "Explore [Category] Roles in [City]" || Layer 4 | "Talk to a [Category] Recruiter" | "Download the Full Guide" || Layer 5 | "Request a Shortlist" | "Schedule a Consultation" |### CTA Rules- Never use generic text like "Click Here," "Learn More," or "Submit"- CTAs must be action-first: start with a verb- Layer 5 CTAs must create mild urgency without being sensationalist (e.g., "Start Your Search Today" — not "Hire Now Before It's Too Late!")- French-language pages: all CTAs must be translated (e.g., "Démarrer votre recherche" not "Start your search")---## 6. Follow these rules without exception.### Linking Hierarchy```Layer 1 (Hub)  ├── Links OUT to: Layer 2, Layer 3, Layer 4, Layer 5  └── Receives links FROM: All other layersLayer 2 (Verticals)  ├── Links OUT to: Relevant Layer 3 pages, Layer 5  └── Links BACK to: Layer 1Layer 3 (Geographic)  ├── Links OUT to: Relevant Layer 2 pages, Layer 5  └── Links BACK to: Layer 1Layer 4 (Resources)  ├── Links OUT to: Layer 1, Layer 5  └── Does NOT link to: Other Layer 4 pages (avoid diluting authority)Layer 5 (Conversion)  └── Links BACK to: Layer 1 only (keep user focused on converting)```### Anchor Text Rules- Anchor text must be **descriptive and keyword-rich** — never "click here" or "this page"- Use the target page's primary keyword as anchor text where natural- Do not repeat the same anchor text more than twice on a single page- Do not force links — if a link doesn't read naturally, do not include it---## 8. CANADIAN MARKET LANGUAGE GUIDELINES### Geographic References- Always specify province alongside city on first mention: "Calgary, Alberta" not just "Calgary"- Use "across Canada" not "nationwide" (more specific to Canadian context)- For remote/hybrid roles use: "remote roles across Canada" or "hybrid positions in [City]"### Industry & Role Terminology- Use Canadian English spelling throughout: "Specialise" → "Specialize," "Organisation" → "Organization," "Colour" → "Color" *(note: Canadian English uses American spellings for most business terms)*- Refer to professional designations by Canadian standard: CPA (not CPA/CA), P.Eng, CHRP- Reference Canadian regulatory bodies where relevant: OSC, OSFI, CRA, IIROC### Salary & Compensation- All compensation figures in **CAD** — always append "CAD" or use the $ symbol with explicit Canadian context- Do not use US Bureau of Labor Statistics data — source from Statistics Canada, Robert Half Canada Salary Guide, or equivalent Canadian sources### French-Language Content (Layer 3: Montreal / Quebec)- All Montreal and Quebec City location pages must be **fully bilingual or fully French** — no hybrid pages- Keyword strategy for French pages must use a **separate French-language keyword pull** (not translated English keywords — Quebec users search differently)- CTA and metadata must be fully translated- Do not use Google Translate output directly — flag French content for human review before publishing---## 9. QUALITY CHECKLIST (AI Self-Verify Before Outputting Any Content)Before returning any generated content, confirm:- [ ] Third-person brand voice used throughout (exceptions noted above)- [ ] No banned words or phrases present- [ ] Content ratio matches the correct layer (see Section 3)- [ ] Word count within the target range for the layer- [ ] H1 follows the correct formula for the layer- [ ] At least one data point or market insight included- [ ] Primary CTA present and uses correct layer language- [ ] Title tag is under 60 characters- [ ] Meta description is under 155 characters- [ ] Internal links follow the hierarchy in Section 7- [ ] All salary/compensation figures are in CAD- [ ] Geographic references include province on first mention- [ ] French pages flagged for human review before publishing---## 10.
